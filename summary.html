The Strategic Case

Why this site, why now, why a sovereign buyer.

Eight arguments for why the Tumwater Brewery site is structurally more valuable to a tribal acquirer than to a conventional private developer.

01

Sovereign Jurisdictional Advantage

Under City of Tumwater jurisdiction, the site is governed by extensive critical area code, frontage exactions, ADA right-of-way standards, and parking minimums that materially constrain the developable program. Under tribal trust status, federal environmental review (NEPA, CWA, ESA, federal historic preservation) governs — but the municipal regulatory layer that drives most of the site’s engineering cost is removed.

02

Strategic Estuary Adjacency

Washington Department of Enterprise Services’ planned removal of the 5th Avenue Dam will restore approximately 260 acres of estuary immediately downstream. The site sits at the south basin gateway — one of the most consequential ecological restorations in the Pacific Northwest, with deep alignment to tribal cultural, treaty fishing, and salmon recovery priorities.

03

Heritage Asset & Brand Equity

The 1906 Olympia Brewhouse is a regionally iconic structure with deep brand recognition across Washington and the Pacific Northwest. The asset carries cultural and commercial weight that does not exist on undeveloped riverfront land — a foundation for hospitality, F&B, event, and destination programming that would take decades to build from scratch.

04

Concept-Validated Program

Geyer Coburn Hutchins’ concept work (D1/D2) demonstrates programmatic feasibility for an integrated hospitality, residential, event, spa, and wedding venue destination, with brewhouse preservation as the anchor. The architectural envelope, vehicular and pedestrian circulation, and parking strategy have already been studied.

05

Residential Overlay Potential

Independent analysis identifies two residential overlay zones supporting between 43 and 130 units at densities ranging from 10 to 30 du/ac, in addition to hospitality and mixed-use program. Recurring residential income provides a stabilizing operating layer underneath the destination components.

06

Riverfront Location & Visibility

Direct frontage on the Deschutes River with adjacency to Tumwater Falls, Tumwater Historical Park, and the Schmidt House. Interstate 5 visibility. Six minutes from Olympia, the Washington state capital. The combination of natural amenity, infrastructure access, and capital-region proximity is structurally rare in Western Washington.

07

Operating Partnership Pathway

AGM’s institutional management platform — with documented experience operating a recently delivered multifamily community for a sovereign tribal client in the Puget Sound region — provides continuity from acquisition through stabilized operations. The seller does not walk away at closing.

08

Generational Land Position

Fee-simple acquisition of a 34-acre Pacific Northwest riverfront assemblage of this scale, with heritage structures, mapped due diligence, and a defined regulatory path, is exceedingly rare. The site does not come back to market on a normal cycle.
