04 The Vision Page 04 of 06

A destination anchored by heritage, layered with hospitality, residential, and event programming.

Geyer Coburn Hutchins’ concept work (D1 and D2) demonstrates a programmatic envelope that preserves the historic brewhouse as the anchor, layers hospitality and event uses around the lower courtyard and river, and locates residential uplands on the eastern overlay. The buyer programs the site — the renderings illustrate feasibility, not prescription.

Program Components

Six layers of program, integrated through one master plan.

Per GCH Concept D1 / D2. Programming and density scale to the buyer’s strategy.

01
Anchor

Brewhouse Preservation & Adaptive Reuse

The 1906 brew tower and adjoining brewhouse structures are preserved and adaptively reused as the cultural and architectural anchor of the site. Programming options include destination restaurant, tasting room, interpretive space, and event venue. The brew tower itself is retained on the City of Tumwater outparcel and remains visible from across the assemblage.

Heritage Preservation Adaptive Reuse Destination F&B Interpretive Programming
02
Hospitality

Hotel, Spa & Hospitality Core

The GCH concept identifies a hospitality program along the lower courtyard, with hotel rooms, a spa wing, pool and amenity deck, and integrated valet access. Programming and key count scale to the buyer’s operational preference. The riverfront location and adjacency to the brewhouse anchor support a flagship boutique or independent hospitality positioning.

Hotel Keys Spa & Wellness Pool & Amenity Deck Valet Arrival
03
Event

Event Center & Wedding Venues

Indoor event space tied to the hospitality core, plus three identified outdoor wedding venue positions across the site (Venue 1 at the upper meadow, Venue 2 at the river bend, Venue 3 on the eastern bluff). The site’s waterfall, river views, and historic brewhouse backdrop create a venue product that does not exist elsewhere in the South Sound.

Indoor Event Center Three Outdoor Venues Waterfall Backdrop Catering & Bar Service
04
Residential

Eastern Overlay Housing

Two identified residential overlay zones support a range of densities. Zone 1 (±1.75 acres, 5–10% slopes) supports 17–52 units. Zone 2 (±2.6 acres, 10–25% slopes, two-tier potential) supports 26–78 units. Combined potential of 43–130 units, depending on density target. Residential income provides a recurring operating layer underneath the destination program.

Zone 1 · 17–52 units Zone 2 · 26–78 units Multi-tier Potential Recurring Operating Income
05
Connectivity

Skybridge or Escalator Access

The concept work studies two principal solutions to the 110-foot vertical drop from Custer Way to the lower brewhouse plateau: a skybridge from the upper plateau into the hospitality core (D1), or a dedicated escalator and inclined elevator system (D2). The vertical connection is one of the defining architectural moves of the project and is a major contributor to guest experience.

Skybridge Option (D1) Escalator Option (D2) Day-Use Parking Above Valet Below
06
Open Space

River Access, Trails & Pedestrian Bridge

The concept retains a pedestrian bridge across the Deschutes connecting the site to Tumwater Historical Park, with kayak and water access at the lower courtyard. An informal trail network and existing City of Tumwater easement along the north edge of Parcel B integrate with the broader Tumwater open space system and the planned estuary trail.
