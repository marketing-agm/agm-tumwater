The site comes with mapped due diligence, candid constraints, and a regulatory path that materially improves under trust status.

KPFF Consulting Engineers prepared a 20-page civil due diligence memorandum (June 2024) covering access, utilities, critical areas, flood zone, contamination, and the implications of the 5th Avenue Dam removal. The summary below presents those findings honestly and reframes each under tribal trust jurisdiction.

Important honesty: placing land into federal trust is a multi-year process administered by the Bureau of Indian Affairs and is not automatic. Timeline, cost, and outcome depend on the tribe’s standing, the nature of the proposed use, and federal review. AGM’s recommendation is that the tribe engage Indian land counsel early to scope feasibility before any binding commitment.

Development Reality

Constraints that remain real, in any scenario.

Some site conditions exist regardless of jurisdiction. These are honest pre-vertical cost items that any acquirer should plan for.

Access

Boston Street SE Descent

The existing access road descends 110 vertical feet at 8–15% grade. Widening to fire apparatus standards (20-ft minimum, 35-ft inside radius, hammerhead turnaround) is required regardless of jurisdiction. KPFF identifies three road section alternatives ranging from 20 to 30 feet wide with corresponding shoring and retaining wall implications. The west pavement edge is currently failing in places.

Utilities

Water, Sewer, Power, Gas

Existing 6-inch cast-iron water service in the driveway requires upgrade to 8-inch looped to deliver minimum fire flow. Sanitary sewer is served by the 2008-upgraded lift station; capacity is adequate per COT. Three-phase overhead power runs down Boston Street. Natural gas extension from Schmidt Place SW (±1,025 LF) is feasible via Puget Sound Energy.

Hydrology

Flood, Stormwater, Wetlands

Site is in FEMA AE zone. Lowest floor elevation must exceed BFE per applicable code. Stormwater discharges direct to the Deschutes (no flow control required, enhanced water quality required). Wetlands along the west and north boundaries require categorization. Elevated structures over crawl space (Snoqualmie City Hall precedent) are a proven flood-resilient design pattern.

Environ.

Contamination & Historic Structures

The site completed a 2002 voluntary cleanup to industrial levels (arsenic, priority metals; source attributed to the historic paint shop). Additional environmental investigation is required to assess any impact from the 2018 fire and 2019 transformer oil spill. Existing structures require condition assessment for adaptive reuse, with structural and seismic upgrades likely.

Flood
Precedent Snoqualmie City Hall — built within the Snoqualmie River floodplain. Landscaped perimeter and porous cladding allow floodwater to rise beneath the occupied floor without impacting building spaces. A proven design pattern for sites in FEMA AE zones.Source: Gensler, via KPFF Falls Development Feasibility Study, June 2024

Order of magnitude: Site infrastructure (access road, utilities, stormwater, environmental remediation, life-safety upgrades to historic structures) is a meaningful pre-vertical line item. Detailed pricing is being prepared with KPFF and GCH and is available to qualified parties under NDA.
